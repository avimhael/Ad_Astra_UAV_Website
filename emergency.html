Oil Spill Tracking

Oil spill incidents can often attract enormous pollution liabilities for responsible parties - one need look no further than the BP Deepwater Horizon incident in 2010 and its associated litigation to appreciate the significance of the pollution risk involved with this industry.

Quickly having eyes on the ground is essential to any effective disaster management plan. However, having them in the air is revolutionary; drones can play a critical role in the minutes and hours after an oil spill by enabling:

> Rapid shoreline monitoring to determine the nature and extent of oiling.
> Examination of the condition of response equipment for maintenance.
> Production of imagery and data to improve response decision-making.
> Monitoring situations where there is a risk to human or animal life.

Damage Assessment

Following natural disasters such as floods, hurricanes, or earthquakes, important infrastructure can become structurally compromised. Drone technology can be utilized in order to detect and map fine scale damage such as cracks, deformations, and shifts. One of the keys to infrastructure damage assessment is timeliness - many natural disasters create dangerous situations that are time-sensitive in nature. The first 24 hours are often critical and current conventional methods can prove inefficient.

Imagery taken before a disaster event can be aligned to those immediately after an event to detect anything that changed. It is recommended that organizations develop a regular inspection routine to prevent catastrophic accidents due to degrading infrastructure.

Flooding Analysis

When flooding occurs the priority response is saving lives and ensuring the safety of inhabitants. However, as events progress it becomes the role of insurance companies to assist their policy holders - a clear and immediate understanding of the scale and likely impact upon insured assets is required.

Aerial surveys can occur before, during, or after a flood event. By using drones it is possible to capture high resolution imagery of the areas affected for flood extent delineation and damage assessments.

Drone technology allows teams to collect data at key locations to help determine whether or not roads need to be closed and to provide emergency staff with essential information for their relief efforts.
