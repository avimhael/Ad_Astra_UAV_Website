Our Story

Ad Astra UAV began while our founder (Matt) was attending Dalhousie University's Faculty of Computer Science and working on developing the Government of New Brunswick's RPAS program. His previous background in geographic information systems, combined with the informational power drone technology allowed, provided drive and vision to form what the organization is today.

Officially forming in 2021, Ad Astra UAV offers a fleet of drones for your project needs and provides services for several professional areas. Our passion for new technologies, client relationships, and a committment to Atlantic Canada serves to drive our success into the future.

Our Brand

Per aspera ad astra (or, less commonly, ad astra per aspera) is a popular Latin phrase meaning 'through hardships to the stars'. The phrase is one of the many Latin sayings that use the expression ad astra, meaning 'to the stars'.

The phrase has origins with Virgil, who wrote in his Aeneid: 'sic itur ad astra' ('thus one journeys to the stars') and 'opta ardua pennis astra sequi' ('desire to pursue the high[hard to reach] stars on wings'). Another origin is Seneca the Younger, who wrote in Hercules: 'non est ad astra mollis e terris via' ('there is no easy way from the earth to the stars').

The Ad Astra UAV branding inspires our desire to 'shoot for the stars', to be brave and adventurous in all things, and to overcome adversity.

Our Drone Fleet

When providing RPAS services, Ad Astra considers client needs carefully in order to apply the right tool for the project.

The DJI Phantom 4 Pro V2.0 delivers when it comes to providing a stable aerial platform. In windy and rainy conditions - or even at high altitudes - you can count on the DJI Phantom to deliver. The sub-250g DJI Mini 2 is light, tiny, and a transformative product for the entire drone industry. This low weight allows it to avoid Transport Canada regulations that drones above 250g would be subject to, and is a valuable and versatile member of the team.

We are continually reaching out to our clients to see how to best suit their current and future needs. Drone technology capable of multispectral imagery and Hollywood-quality video are forthcoming for the Ad Astra toolkit.
