2D & 3D Modeling

A 2D or 3D model for your project can provide a variety of benefits. By allowing you to see an exact representation of the terrain where you’ll be building or the existing structure that you’ll be renovating, a model can help you make informed decisions based on complete visibility.

Accuracy

Drones can fly at extremely low altitudes and take high quality imagery, producing results that are highly accurate and precise.

Precision

High resolution orthophotos enable surveyors to perform highly-accurate distance and surface measurements, such as for volume or slope.

Save on Cost

Capturing topographic data with a drone is up to five times faster than with land-based methods and requires less manpower.

Exploration

A mapping drone can take off and fly almost anywhere - you are no longer limited by unreachable areas, unsafe slopes or harsh terrain unsuitable for traditional measuring techniques.

Plan

Imagery produced from drones allow planners to examine the existing social and environmental conditions of a particular site and consider the impact of different scenarios.

Analyze

Drones can provide complex analysis for a number of industries, such as calculating potential yield for agriculture and boundary estimates for real estate.

Agriculture & Forestry

Drones provide a way to gather consistent datasets with similar details to field-based monitoring data. It is recommended that land managers utilize UAV image-based monitoring for agriculture and forestry assets that require targeted monitoring. Deliverables can identify and continuously monitor trends in asset condition, health, and degradation related to land use policies and management strategies.

The science of photogrammetry is a reliable way to estimate biomass, landcover, and ecosystem productivity, with several potential cost-saving implications for conservation and agricultural science.

Construction Site Monitoring

The construction industry has seen a huge benefit from drone imagery, mapping, and 3D modeling software. Construction firms can monitor the progress of their construction site with regular updates, and they can easily inspect different areas of the site. The accuracy of the 3D models also means they can be used to calculate distance, area, and volume measurements.

Whether you schedule daily, weekly, or monthly updates for your project, modeling via drone imagery can provide useful progress updates to the development team and investors.

Inspections

Take your asset inspections to the next level with Ad Astra UAV's data modeling services.

Using drones, inspections can view difficult angles of properties and equipment more safely and efficiently. They can spot potential problems with the detailed 3D models, add annotations, and see the original imagery for a better look at a particular area.

Many inspection assets are larger, more dangerous, or in challenging to reach locations. Inspecting them safely at the office with 3D models can help ensure their longevity and safe operation (as well as employee safety).

Architecture & Engineering

Utilizing 3D modeling solutions, architecture and engineering firms are creating realistic and detailed context models for their buildings and development projects. Architects use these to present how their finished project will look and to present their project to investors and key stakeholders.

While using satellite imagery for site planning is common among architects, these visuals are often available in low resolution and produce less accurate data. Data collected by drones can completely eliminate the need for hiring land surveyors for creating topographic surveys. Instead, architects can use this information to build accurate 3D models of the terrain and site and import them directly into drafting and modeling software.

Public Safety

Public safety utilizes 3D modeling technology for documenting and recreating crime scenes, recording evidence, and providing a clearer picture for investigators, forensics, and jury members. Drones can compile an accurate view of an actual crime scene, allowing details to be noticed well after the initial analysis is complete.
